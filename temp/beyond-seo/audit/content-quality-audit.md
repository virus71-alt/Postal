# Content Quality and E-E-A-T Audit

This file defines how Beyond SEO evaluates content quality, topical authority, helpfulness, trust, and E-E-A-T.

Content quality is not “more words.” It is whether the page gives users and search systems the information, proof, and confidence needed to choose the business.

---

## 1. Purpose

The content audit answers:

```text
Is this content useful enough, specific enough, trustworthy enough, and complete enough to rank and convert?
```

---

## 2. Content Types to Audit

```text
Homepage
Service pages
Product pages
Course pages
Location pages
Blog/support articles
FAQ/help content
Author/expert pages
Case studies
Testimonials/reviews pages
Comparison pages
Cost/pricing pages
Certification/credential pages
```

---

## 3. Core Content Quality Criteria

Check every important page for:

```text
Specificity
Search intent match
Originality
Depth
Clarity
Expertise
Trust proof
Freshness
Local relevance if needed
Conversion support
Internal linking
AEO answer readiness
```

---

## 4. Helpful Content Questions

Ask:

```text
Does this page solve the user’s real question?
Does it explain what the user should do next?
Does it contain original insight or proof?
Does it feel written for humans or just search engines?
Does it answer objections before the CTA?
Does it have enough detail for the decision?
Is it better than competitor pages?
```

If answer is no, content needs improvement.

---

## 5. E-E-A-T Framework

E-E-A-T means:

```text
Experience
Expertise
Authoritativeness
Trustworthiness
```

### Experience

Check:

```text
Real-world examples
Case studies
Before/after proof where compliant
Screenshots
Project examples
Stories
Actual process details
```

### Expertise

Check:

```text
Expert credentials
Doctor/instructor/founder proof
Author/reviewer identity
Accurate explanations
No overclaims
```

### Authoritativeness

Check:

```text
Backlinks
Citations
Mentions
Memberships
Certifications
Media references
Recognizable proof
Topical depth
```

### Trustworthiness

Check:

```text
Clear contact information
Policies
Disclaimers
Authentic reviews
Transparent pricing/financing where useful
Realistic claims
Secure forms/payment
```

## 6. YMYL Content Rules

YMYL content requires extra care.

YMYL includes:

```text
Medical
Dental
Legal
Financial
Safety/security
Government/civic
High-stakes education/career claims
```

Required for YMYL pages:

```text
Expert/doctor/reviewer credentials
Reviewed-by or medically reviewed note where appropriate
Last updated/reviewed date
Clear disclaimers
Risks/limitations
No exaggerated claims
No fake outcomes
Sources/citations where needed
Clear contact/business info
```

For medical/dental service pages, include:

```text
Candidacy
Procedure/process
Risks
Recovery
Cost/financing
Alternatives
Doctor credentials
Reviews/proof
Appointment CTA
```

---

## 7. Thin Content Audit

Thin content is not just low word count.

Thin content means:

```text
Too little useful information for the intent.
```

Signs:

```text
Service page under 400-500 words in a competitive niche
No process explanation
No cost/price guidance where relevant
No FAQs
No proof/trust signals
No internal links
Generic text copied across services
No local relevance
No unique expert insight
Only sales copy, no useful detail
```

Fix:

```text
Expand with useful sections, not filler.
```

## 9. Topical Authority Audit

Check whether the website has clusters.

A strong cluster includes:

```text
Hub page / money page
Supporting articles
FAQs/PAA content
Comparison pages
Cost/process pages
Internal links
Schema
Expert proof
Backlinks/mentions where possible
```

Example dental implant cluster:

```text
/dental-implants/
/all-on-4-dental-implants/
/all-on-5-dental-implants/
/dental-implant-cost/
/am-i-a-candidate-for-dental-implants/
/dental-implant-recovery/
/dental-implants-vs-dentures/
```

Example AI course cluster:

```text
/ai-automation-course/
/ai-agents-course/
/prompt-engineering-course-pakistan/
/ai-course-pakistan/
/what-is-ai-automation/
/n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier/
/ai-agents-for-business/
```

---

## 10. Duplicate and Generic Content Audit

Check:

```text
Duplicate service descriptions
Same title/meta across pages
Same intro across locations
AI-generated generic content
Copied manufacturer/course descriptions
Thin city pages with swapped city names
Repeated FAQ blocks
```

Fix:

```text
Add unique local/service proof
Add specific examples
Add real process
Add expert commentary
Add case/project examples
Rewrite repeated sections
```

---

## 11. Freshness Audit

Check:

```text
Last updated date
Old stats
Outdated tool names/prices
Old screenshots
Old policy references
Old treatment/course details
Broken examples
Stale blog archive
```

High-priority freshness:

```text
AI tools/courses
Medical procedures
Legal/financial content
Pricing/cost content
Technology/software
News/trends pages
```

---

## 12. AEO/GEO Content Readiness

Check if content has:

```text
Direct answer blocks
Definitions
Step-by-step sections
Tables/comparisons
Cost/process/risk answers
FAQs
Entity summaries
Author/expert credibility
Sources/citations
Schema
```

Answer engines prefer content that is clear, specific, and easy to extract.

---

## 13. Content Scoring

Score each page out of 10:

```text
Intent match: 0-2
Depth/completeness: 0-2
Specificity/originality: 0-2
Trust/E-E-A-T: 0-2
Conversion/internal links: 0-2
```

Labels:

```text
8-10: Strong
6-7: Improve
4-5: Weak
0-3: Critical rebuild
```

## 16. Common Mistakes

Do not:

```text
Add words just to increase word count.
Create random blogs without internal links.
Create doorway city pages.
Use fake author profiles.
Use fake reviews or medical claims.
Copy competitor content.
Overuse FAQs as a shortcut.
Ignore conversion.
Ignore expert proof for YMYL.
```

---

## 17. Final Content Quality Gate

A page is content-strong when:

```text
It matches search intent.
It is specific.
It answers real objections.
It shows expertise and proof.
It links to related pages.
It has clear next action.
It can be understood by search engines and answer engines.
```
