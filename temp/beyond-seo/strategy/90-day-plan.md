# 90-Day SEO Ranking and Growth Plan

The 90-day plan turns the audit into a complete execution roadmap.

It should be realistic, business-focused, and measurable. It must not promise rankings.

---

## 1. Purpose

The 90-day plan answers:

```text
What exactly happens across three months?
What gets fixed, built, linked, tracked, and measured?
What movement is expected?
What remains a longer-term project?
```

---

## 2. Three-Month Structure

### Month 1: Foundation

```text
Tracking
Critical technical fixes
Existing winner improvements
Highest-value page improvements
GBP basics
Schema basics
Conversion path fixes
```

### Month 2: Expansion

```text
Missing money pages
Support content clusters
Internal link architecture
Local SEO expansion
Backlink prospecting
AEO/GEO answer blocks
```

### Month 3: Authority and Optimization

```text
Link acquisition
Content refresh based on GSC
CTR improvements
Competitor gap closure
Conversion testing
AEO/GEO strengthening
Monthly reporting
```

## 4. Deliverable Categories

Every 90-day plan should include:

```text
Technical fixes
On-page improvements
Content/page builds
Internal linking
Schema
Local SEO
Backlinks/authority
AEO/GEO
Conversion tracking
Reporting
```

```text
Organic landing page sessions

Form submissions

WhatsApp clicks
GBP calls/directions
New referring domains
Indexed pages

```

---

## 6. Expected Movement Language

Use honest language:

```text
Expected movement:
- Faster movement possible on existing ranking pages and low-competition long-tail terms.
- Competitive money keywords may require stronger authority, content depth, and local trust.
- Rankings are not guaranteed, but this plan increases the probability of measurable growth.
```

Avoid:

```text
Guaranteed page 1 in 90 days.
```

---

## 7. Rule

The 90-day plan must be executable.

Each task should have:

```text
What
Why
Owner
Priority
KPI
```
